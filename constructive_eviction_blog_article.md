<h2 id="plain-language">What “constructive eviction” means (plain language)</h2>
<p>People use “constructive eviction” to describe a situation where a tenant is <em>functionally forced out</em> because the home becomes unlivable or because management conduct makes normal occupancy unrealistic.</p>

<p>In practice, the concept usually involves four elements:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Unlivable conditions or coercive conduct</strong> (not just inconvenience).</li>
  <li><strong>Landlord responsibility</strong> (action or inaction).</li>
  <li><strong>Notice + documentation</strong> (a clean record showing you raised issues and requested correction/evidence).</li>
  <li><strong>Displacement</strong> (you leave because of the conditions, not unrelated reasons).</li>
</ul>

<p>This article stays in the lane of <strong>documentation and evidence hygiene</strong>: how I kept my record clean, chronological, and verifiable.</p>

<h2 id="evidence">My evidence system (primary-only, exhibit-ready)</h2>
<p>If you do nothing else, do this: <strong>turn chaos into a timestamped record</strong>. Here is the system I used.</p>

<h3>1) One master log (timeline-first)</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>One row per event</strong>, in chronological order.</li>
  <li>Each row includes: <em>date/time, actor, event, exact quote (≤25 words), source file, tag</em>.</li>
  <li>If it’s not documented (no screenshot/email/audio), I mark it as <strong>User-reported</strong> until I capture proof.</li>
</ul>

<h3>2) A “primary-source only” folder</h3>
<p>I separate documents into two buckets so I never confuse analysis for evidence:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Primary</strong>: emails, notices, screenshots, recordings, official filing confirmations.</li>
  <li><strong>Secondary</strong>: my summaries, drafts, strategy notes, and writeups.</li>
</ul>

<h3>3) File naming convention</h3>
<pre><code>YYYY-MM-DD__CATEGORY__ACTOR__SHORT-DESCRIPTION.ext

Examples:
2026-02-09__PORTAL-FEE__PROPERTY-MGMT__CHARGE-SCREENSHOT.png
2026-01-31__EMAIL__VENDOR__RENT-CONFIRMATION-THREAT.pdf
2026-02-16__VIDEO__SECURITY__CAMERA-CLIP.mp4</code></pre>

<h3>4) Weekly exhibit packet habit</h3>
<p>Once a week, I export a single PDF called <strong>Exhibit_Packet_YYYY-MM-DD.pdf</strong> with:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Exhibit index (1 page)</li>
  <li>Timeline highlights (1–2 pages)</li>
  <li>Portal fee screenshots</li>
  <li>Key emails/notices (headers preserved privately)</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="people-first">People-first gate: how I kept this fair and publish-safe</h2>
<ul>
  <li>I describe actions and documents, not personal attacks.</li>
  <li>I avoid publishing private identifiers (unit number, phone, financial info).</li>
  <li>I label anything without documentary support as <strong>User-reported</strong>.</li>
  <li>I keep “primary evidence” separate from my interpretations.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ (documentation-focused)</h2>

<h3>What screenshots matter most?</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Charges with dates, amounts, and descriptions.</li>
  <li>Any “late/legal” fees and when they were posted.</li>
  <li>Any portal messages referencing court/eviction/collections.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What should I save from email?</h3>
<ul>
  <li>The full message (not just cropped screenshots).</li>
  <li>Preserve full headers privately when possible.</li>
</ul>

<h3>How do I keep my record calm and credible?</h3>
<p>Use neutral labels (“Charge posted,” “Evidence requested,” “No evidence produced”), quote the document, and stay chronological.</p>
